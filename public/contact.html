Common questions

Asked before every first session

Is the first consultation really free?

Yes, and there is no obligation attached to it. We would rather tell you honestly that a plan will not work than take a fee and let you find out eight months later.

How long does it take from first session to visa?

Three to six months is typical, but it depends on the country and how quickly attestation clears. Germany and Poland tend to run longer because of the document chain; the UK is usually fastest once the file is complete.

What happens if my visa is refused?

We read the refusal notice with you, identify the specific ground, and re-file at no additional charge on cases we handled. Most refusals come down to funds evidence or an inconsistency in the documents rather than academic weakness.

Do you handle attestation, or do I go myself?

We handle it. IBCC equivalence, HEC attestation, Ministry of Foreign Affairs legalisation and embassy stamps are all done by our own staff. You do not need to visit those offices.

Can you get me a scholarship?

We can identify what you are eligible for and help you write a competitive application, but nobody can guarantee an award. We will tell you plainly whether your marks are in the running before you spend time on it.

I already have an offer letter. Can you just do the visa?

Yes. Bring the offer, your financial documents and any attestation already completed, and we will take it from there.
