How we work

Six habits we do not bend on

We tell you no

If your marks or funds do not support the country you are asking about, you hear it in the first session — not after you have paid.

Deadlines drive the plan

Attestation and applications run in parallel, not one after the other, because a missed intake costs you a year.

Shortlists, not sales

Universities are chosen on your entry requirements and budget. We will name the ones paying us commission if you ask.

Attestation in-house

HEC, IBCC, embassy legalisation and certified translation are handled by our own staff, not subcontracted out.

One person owns your file

You get a named case officer and their direct WhatsApp. No switchboard, no repeating your situation to a new person each time.

Support past the airport

Accommodation booked before departure, a pre-flight briefing, and someone to call in the first month abroad.

Document attestation

The part that quietly costs students an entire intake

Every foreign university and embassy wants your Pakistani documents verified through a specific chain. Skip a step and the file is returned. We run all four stages in-house.

IBCC equivalence

Converts your Matric, FSc or A-Level results into the grading system the receiving country recognises.

7–14 working days
HEC attestation

Verification of degrees and transcripts by the Higher Education Commission. Required by nearly every university abroad.

10–20 working days
Ministry of Foreign Affairs

State-level legalisation of the attested documents, which most embassies will not proceed without.

3–7 working days
Embassy & translation

Final legalisation at the destination country's embassy, plus certified translation where the language requires it.

5–15 working days
